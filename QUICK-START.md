## Hour 3-8: Create First Lyric Video

### Style: Terminal Aesthetic (fastest + most authentic)

**Why this style:**
- Matches cyberpunk theme perfectly
- Can be done in 2-3 hours once you have template
- Free tools available
- Looks intentional, not low-budget

**Tools needed (all free):**
- Video editor: DaVinci Resolve (free) or CapCut (easier, also free)
- Terminal font: "Fira Code" or "JetBrains Mono" (download free from Google Fonts)
- Background: Black with subtle scan lines

**Step-by-step:**

1. **Get the song audio from Suno**
   - Export as MP3 or WAV
   - Duration: ~3 minutes

2. **Create assets** (30 min):
   - Open text editor, type all lyrics with timing marks
   - Download background: Search "matrix code falling" or use pure black
   - Get glitch effect overlays (search "VHS glitch overlay free")

3. **In video editor** (2-3 hours):

   **Setup:**
   - Create 1920x1080 project
   - Import song audio
   - Set black background

   **Intro (0:00-0:08):**
   - 0:00-0:03: Black screen with text appearing: `$ who(s)we --present`
   - 0:03-0:05: Glitch effect
   - 0:05-0:08: Song title types out: `> Electric_Dreams.mp3`

   **Lyrics (0:08-end):**
   - Use white/cyan text (terminal green also works)
   - Mono-spaced font (Fira Code or Courier New)
   - Lyrics appear line-by-line synchronized to vocals
   - Section labels in different color: `[Verse 1]` in cyan, lyrics in white
   - Add subtle cursor blink after each line
   - Optional: Previous lines slightly fade/dim as new ones appear

   **Visual rhythm:**
   - Chorus: Text slightly larger or brighter
   - Bridge: Text in center instead of left-aligned
   - Add subtle scan line effect (horizontal lines moving down)

   **Outro (last 5 seconds):**
   - Lyrics fade
   - Text appears: `$ visit whoswe.xyz`
   - Fade to black with: `Subscribe for more` in corner

4. **Export settings:**
   - Format: MP4
   - Resolution: 1080p (1920x1080)
   - Frame rate: 30fps
   - Bitrate: High quality

**Time: 3-5 hours (first video will be slower, template makes future ones faster)**

## Hour 25-48: Analytics & Next Steps

### Day 2-3: Check What's Working

**YouTube Studio:**
- Views in first 24 hours: Anything over 50 is good for brand new channel
- Click-through rate: Over 4% is strong
- Watch time: Did people watch past first 30 seconds?

- Completion rate: Over 60% is solid
- Profile visits: Are people clicking through?
- Link clicks: Are they going to YouTube?

**Reddit:**
- Upvote ratio: Over 80% means good reception
- Comments: Quality > quantity
- Did anyone cross-post or share?

## When Something Goes Wrong

**"My video got 5 views and they're all me"**
- Normal for first 48 hours on brand new channel
- Keep posting, engage elsewhere, drive traffic from TikTok/Reddit
- YouTube algorithm needs data

**"No one is engaging on TikTok"**
- Try posting at different time (test morning vs evening)
- Test Clip 5 (philosophical question) - conversation starters perform better than pure music
- Make sure first 1 second is visually striking

**"Reddit downvoted me"**
- Did you read the subreddit rules?
- Did your post feel spammy vs. authentic?
- Try r/singularity - they're more receptive to experimental AI content
- Wait 48 hours, try different subreddit with adjusted tone

**"I can't make the video look good"**
- Terminal aesthetic is SUPPOSED to look simple
- Black background + white text + timing to music = totally sufficient
- Look at lo-fi hip hop videos - simplicity works
- Don't let perfect be the enemy of shipped

**"This is taking way longer than estimated"**
- First video always does
- Template makes it 10x faster next time
- If you're stuck on video after 8 hours, simplify: just black screen + white lyrics + timing
- Shipping matters more than polish
